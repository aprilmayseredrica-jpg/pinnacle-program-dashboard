The Strategic Imperative: A Deep Dive into VA Resignations

Our analysis of all 327 VA resignations reveals a clear and powerful story. The reasons VAs leave change dramatically with tenure. While early departures are a mix of preventable issues, long-term departures are a sign of success. These seasoned VAs are not leaving because they are unhappy; they are leaving because they are ready for the next step in their career—a step only the Pinnacle Program can provide. This is our single greatest opportunity to improve retention, increase revenue, and fulfill our brand promise.

Analysis of Key Drivers

  • Not Specified / Others (218 VAs)

    This is our largest category and our biggest opportunity. For long-term VAs, this often serves as a discreet reason for accepting a direct-hire offer from their client. The Pinnacle Program provides a formal, transparent alternative.

  • Better Opportunities (53 VAs)

    Our VAs are high-quality talent. This program makes the best opportunity—a direct role with their trusted client—a path that runs *through* MMM, not around us.

  • Personal/Health (35 VAs)

    These are unavoidable life events. Our focus remains on the preventable departures.

  • Performance/Behavior (21 VAs)

    This is the smallest category, proving that the vast majority of VAs who resign are valuable, proven assets worth retaining.

  • ✔MMM WINS: By converting a retention loss into a high-margin revenue event and strengthening the client relationship for future business.
