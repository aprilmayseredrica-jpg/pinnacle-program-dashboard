The Buyout Calendar: A Strategic Forecast

Our data reveals clear seasonal trends. By aligning these with the typical business cycles of US medical clients and the cultural-economic factors of the Philippine VA market, we can anticipate when buyout conversations are most likely to occur and prepare our Client Success team for proactive engagement.

Q1

Jan - Mar: The Strategic Start

US clients finalize new annual budgets, while top Filipino VAs become active job-seekers after receiving their legally mandated "13th month pay" in December. This creates a perfect storm for strategic buyouts. Peak Buyout Month: January.

Q2

Apr - Jun: Performance & Confirmation

Post-Q1 performance reviews in the US confirm the VA's value and ROI. This is a key trigger for clients to convert a successful placement into a permanent hire before mid-year budget adjustments. Peak Buyout Month: May.

Q3

Jul - Sep: Mid-Year Planning

As clients reassess mid-year budgets and plan for Q4, securing essential personnel becomes a top priority, creating another buyout window before the end-of-year rush. Peak Buyout Month: August.

The Two Stories of VA Resignation

This analysis of 418 resignations reveals a critical insight: the reasons VAs leave change dramatically with tenure. The chart opposite visualizes this shift, making the case for a targeted retention strategy.

The Narrative: Under 1 Year

Early resignations are a mix of preventable issues. While many VAs seek other opportunities, a notable portion leaves due to tangible performance problems. This is where we must improve our screening and early-support processes.

The Narrative: After 1 Year

The story flips completely for long-term VAs. Negative performance issues become statistically insignificant. The overwhelming driver is the search for "Better Opportunities," strongly indicating that these proven VAs are being pursued for direct-hire roles—the exact scenario the Pinnacle Program is designed for.
