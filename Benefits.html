A Snapshot of Mover Sentiment

This report analyzes feedback from the VA Benefits Survey conducted on September 11-12, 2025. The high participation rate underscores our VAs' trust and engagement, providing us with critical insights to shape a more effective and profitable benefits strategy for 2026.

Key Survey Findings

The $100 VA Bond is seen as a reasonable commitment tool.

The vast majority of VAs understand the policy's purpose of encouraging commitment, with very few finding it stressful or unfair. The policy is functioning as intended.

The Anniversary Bonus is a powerful and highly valued retention driver.

Feedback is overwhelmingly positive. VAs feel genuinely appreciated and motivated to build long-term careers with MMM, confirming this incentive is a cornerstone of our retention strategy.

VAs are excited for the Christmas Bonus but feel the amounts are too low.

While the introduction of a Christmas Bonus is appreciated, the current amounts are the single biggest opportunity for improvement identified in the survey. Many VAs compare it to the "13th-month pay" cultural standard.

VAs have strong confidence that their feedback is heard and valued.

This high level of trust is a significant asset for MMM. It fosters a positive culture and indicates that our channels for communication are effective. This survey is a testament to that trust.

AM-VA engagement is effective for some but misses the mark for others.

While many VAs feel connected, a notable portion feels the activities are routine or irrelevant, especially those on solo accounts. This suggests a need for a more inclusive and varied engagement strategy.

2026 Budget Proposal: Driving Value Through Strategic Reallocation

Recap: Key Insights

  • Christmas Bonus: The #1 opportunity. VAs desire an amount that feels more substantial, akin to a "13th-month pay."
  • Tenured VA Loyalty: Long-term VAs feel undervalued when new hires receive higher rates, indicating a need to better reward loyalty.
  • High-Value Perks: VAs are asking for better healthcare options (especially for dependents) and the ability to convert unused leave to cash.
  • Engagement ROI: The current engagement budget isn't delivering consistent value across the entire VA population.

Profitable & Value-Driven Recommendations

The 2026 strategy is to reallocate funds from low-impact initiatives to high-value incentives. This creates a positive, profitable cycle of retention and growth without increasing the overall budget.
