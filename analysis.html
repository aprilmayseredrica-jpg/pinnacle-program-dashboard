In-depth Analysis

A deep dive into the open-ended feedback reveals the most impactful areas for improvement.

Top Requested Benefits: A Pareto Analysis

The top 4 requests from our VAs account for over 80% of open-text mentions. Focusing on these priorities will yield the highest return on investment in VA satisfaction.

*Top Requests: 1) More Frequent Bonuses, 2) Improved HMO/Health Benefits, 3) Higher Christmas Bonus, 4) Tenure-Based Salary Increases.

Analysis: The Power of Frequency & Loyalty

The Pareto chart clearly shows that **more frequent bonuses** are the top priority for VAs, with **193 mentions**. This indicates that annual bonuses, while appreciated, are not enough to drive consistent motivation. VAs are looking for more regular recognition for their hard work.

Furthermore, a significant portion of feedback points to a sense of inequity for our tenured VAs. Many feel their long-term commitment is not rewarded proportionally to the starting salaries of new hires. Addressing this **tenure-based salary increase** is critical for long-term retention and morale.

Financial Analysis & Proposed Initiatives

Our proposals are backed by financial projections to ensure they are both impactful and sustainable.

Proposal: Adjust Christmas Bonus

✍️ Reasoning: It's worth it to adjust the bonus *before* it's even given out. The survey shows that 16% of VAs are neutral or unenthusiastic about the current bonus amount. A modest increase can significantly boost morale and create a powerful first impression, ensuring the bonus is a true motivator.

Financial Breakdown:

  • ✍️ Projected 2025 Cost (Current Plan): $63,200
  • ✍️ Projected 2025 Cost (Proposed Flat $100): $72,900 (729 VAs x $100)
  • ✍️ Incremental Cost: $9,700

This small investment is projected to have a large return in morale and loyalty, converting a neutral group into an enthusiastic one.

Proposal: Reallocate AM-VA Engagement Fund

✍️ Reasoning: VAs overwhelmingly requested more frequent bonuses tied to performance. We can fund this by reallocating the AM-VA Engagement fund, which the survey indicates is perceived as less valuable. This is a strategic trade-off.

Financial Breakdown:

  • ✍️ Projected AM-VA Engagement Fund (EOY 2025): $7,987.39
  • ✍️ Proposed Quarterly Attendance Bonus: Award $25 to 50 VAs per quarter.
  • ✍️ Projected Annual Cost: $5,000 (200 VAs x $25)

By reallocating this fund, we save over $2,900 annually while directly addressing a top VA request. This is a cost-effective way to provide more consistent and impactful recognition.

Proposal: Formalize Tenured VA Loyalty

✍️ Reasoning: Our most-loyal VAs are concerned that their tenure is not being adequately rewarded with salary increases. This proposal creates a clear path for growth and acknowledges their long-term commitment.

How to Implement:

  • ✍️ Guaranteed Salary Review: Introduce an annual salary review policy for all VAs with **2+ years of tenure**. This provides a clear, predictable path for compensation growth.
  • ✍️ Enhanced Anniversary Merch: For VAs reaching their **3rd, 4th, or 5+ year anniversaries**, offer a choice of higher-value merchandise or a cash-in-lieu option to make their loyalty feel more substantial.

Anticipated Questions & Direct Answers

  • Q: Why not just give out merch at the Summit to save on shipping?

    ✍️ A: This could backfire. The survey feedback shows that the Anniversary Incentive is a key motivator. Limiting its fulfillment to an event that not all VAs can attend risks making a significant portion of our VAs feel excluded. The small cost savings of **$1,200** annually do not justify the potential negative impact on morale and retention.

  • Q: How do we address the HMO feedback?

    ✍️ A: The data indicates that 21% of VAs feel our current HMO coverage is insufficient, especially for those in provinces. We should investigate a provider that offers better, more accessible coverage or a flexible benefit that VAs can use for other health-related expenses.
