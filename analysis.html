Financial Analysis & Proposed Initiatives

Our proposals are backed by financial projections to ensure they are both impactful and sustainable.

Top Requested Benefits: A Pareto Analysis

The top 4 requests from our VAs account for over 80% of open-text mentions.

*Top Requests: 1) More Frequent Bonuses, 2) Improved HMO/Health Benefits, 3) Higher Christmas Bonus, 4) Tenure-Based Salary Increases.

Proposal: Adjust Christmas Bonus

**Reasoning:** The chart above shows that 16% of VAs are neutral or unenthusiastic about the current bonus amount, even before receiving it. A modest increase can significantly boost morale.

Financial Breakdown:

  • **Projected 2025 Cost (Current Plan):** **$63,200**
  • **Projected 2025 Cost (Proposed Flat $100):** **$72,900** (729 VAs x $100)
  • **Incremental Cost:** **$9,700**

This small investment is projected to have a large return in morale and loyalty, converting a neutral group into an enthusiastic one.

Proposal: Reallocate AM-VA Engagement Fund

**Reasoning:** VAs overwhelmingly requested more frequent bonuses tied to performance. We can fund this by reallocating the AM-VA Engagement fund, which is perceived as less valuable.

Financial Breakdown:

  • **Projected AM-VA Engagement Fund (EOY 2025):** **$7,987.39**
  • **Proposed Quarterly Attendance Bonus:** Award **$25** to **50 VAs** per quarter.
  • **Projected Annual Cost:** **$5,000** (200 VAs x $25)

This re-allocation is a cost-effective way to directly address a top VA request, providing more consistent and impactful recognition.

Proposal: Formalize Tenured VA Loyalty

**Reasoning:** The most-loyal VAs are concerned that their tenure is not being adequately rewarded with salary increases. This proposal creates a clear path for growth and acknowledges their long-term commitment.

How to Implement:

  • **Guaranteed Salary Review:** Introduce an annual salary review policy for all VAs with **2+ years of tenure**. This provides a clear, predictable path for compensation growth.
  • **Enhanced Anniversary Merch:** For VAs reaching their **3rd, 4th, or 5+ year anniversaries**, offer a choice of higher-value merchandise or a cash-in-lieu option to make their loyalty feel more substantial.

Anticipated Questions & Direct Answers

  • Q: Why not just give out merch at the Summit to save on shipping?

    **A:** The Anniversary Incentive is a key motivator. Limiting its fulfillment to an event that not all VAs can attend risks making a significant portion of our VAs feel excluded. The small cost savings of **$1,200** annually do not justify the potential negative impact on morale and retention.

  • Q: How do we address the HMO feedback?

    **A:** The data indicates that **21% of VAs (154 mentions)** feel our current HMO coverage is insufficient. We should investigate a provider that offers better, more accessible coverage or a flexible benefit that VAs can use for other health-related expenses.
